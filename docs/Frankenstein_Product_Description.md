# Frankenstein — Product Description

## The Problem

AI agents — programs that can read documents, crunch numbers, make decisions, and write reports on their own — are useful in almost every industry. But building one is hard. You need someone who understands the business problem (a domain expert) and someone who knows how to wire up AI tools (an engineer). These are rarely the same person.

So what happens today? A loan officer who wants an AI assistant to help review applications has to explain the process to an engineering team. That team spends weeks learning the domain, picking the right tools, writing code, testing it, fixing it. A procurement manager who wants AI to score suppliers goes through the same cycle. Every new agent is a custom engineering project. It takes too long, costs too much, and most teams just don't have the engineers to spare.

## What Frankenstein Does

Frankenstein lets the domain expert build the agent themselves — no engineering team needed.

You open a chat. You describe what you need. Frankenstein asks you a few sharp questions: what data comes in, what should come out, what rules matter, what a good result looks like. Then it does the engineering for you — picks the right tools, designs how the agents should work together, checks its own design for problems, writes the code, runs it, and fixes what breaks.

Before anything gets built, you see the blueprint. You can read it, change it, or approve it. You stay in control. You just don't have to do the engineering.

What comes out the other end is a real, working multi-agent system — not a prototype, not a demo. It runs on the same frameworks professional engineers use.

## Who Is This For

People who know their domain cold but don't write code:

- A compliance officer who needs an agent to scan regulation changes and flag what matters
- A supply chain manager who wants suppliers scored and ranked automatically
- An underwriter who needs AI to pull data from loan documents and assess risk
- An analyst who wants to automate a weekly report that currently takes two days

Anyone with a clear workflow they want automated but no way to build it themselves.

## Why It Matters

There are more AI agent use cases than there are engineers to build them. That's the bottleneck. Not the technology — the people.

Frankenstein removes the people bottleneck. The domain expert talks to it like they'd talk to an engineer, and it does the rest. The output isn't dumbed down — it's the same quality an engineering team would deliver, just faster.

One conversation. Working agents. Tested and ready.
